Nirvana is an American rock band formed in 1987 in Aberdeen, Washington by Kurt Cobain and Krist Novoselic. The band had a huge influence on the development of grunge music and became one of the key alternative rock bands of the 1990s. Their music combined elements of punk, rock, and pop music, and their lyrics often touched on themes of alienation, depression, and social problems.

Nirvana's first album, *Bleach*, was released in 1989 on the independent label Sub Pop and brought the band recognition in the underground scene. However, real success came with the release of *Nevermind* in 1991, which featured their biggest hit, **Smells Like Teen Spirit**. The album changed the music industry and made Nirvana one of the most popular bands of the time.

Despite their success, the band only lasted a few years. In 1994, Kurt Cobain committed suicide, ending Nirvana's existence. However, their legacy lives on, and their music continues to inspire millions around the world.
